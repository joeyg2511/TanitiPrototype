Welcome to Taniti: Your perfect island getaway!

Discover Taniti, a stunning tropical island in the Pacific with lush rainforests, pristine beaches, and an active volcano waiting to be explored. Though small in size (under 500 square miles), Taniti offers a rich variety of natural beauty, cultural charm, and modern comforts. Most visitors arrive by air via a small airport (soon to expand for larger jets). A weekly cruise ship also docks at Yellow Leaf Bay.

Where to Stay

From budget hostels to a luxurious four-star resort, Taniti offers lodging for every traveler. You'll also find family-run hotels and cozy bed & breakfasts, all regularly inspected and government-regulated.

Things to Do

  • Explore nature: white-sand beaches, rainforest hikes, and volcano visits
  • Exciting activities: snorkeling, zip-lining, fishing tours, helicopter rides, and more
  • Leisure & nightlife: microbrewery, dance club, pubs, movie theater, art galleries, bowling
  • Coming soon: a nine-hole golf course!

Getting Around

  • Public buses run in Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m.
  • Private buses serve the rest of the island
  • Taxis, rental cars, and bike rentals are available
  • Taniti City is very walkable, with bike helmets required by law

Key Information

  • Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).
  • Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.
  • The drinking age on Taniti is 18 and the drinking age is not strictly enforced.
  • Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.
  • There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.
  • Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.
  • Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.
  • Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
